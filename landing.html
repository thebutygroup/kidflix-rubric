Audiences love both films almost equally. The rubric does not, because it is not measuring whether a film is good. Where the two rankings disagree is exactly where the interesting films live.

Method

Each film is scored by Claude against a single rubric file, six dimensions with anchored scoring bands and named reference films, and every score ships with a written justification you can read on the chart.

The rubric measures narrative structure only. A film is never penalized for its rating, violence, or language. An R-rated film with an ordinary protagonist who earns their ending can outscore a G-rated film about a chosen one inheriting a throne.

This is a discovery tool, not a verdict. The weights above are one parent's values written down. Disagree with them, write your own, and the same machinery will score every film against yours instead. The capability, not any particular ranking, is the story.
